Professional Ceiling Repair in Texas

Ceiling repairs present unique challenges that wall repairs don't — gravity, panel weight, overhead access, and the fact that lighting hits ceilings at a flat angle that makes every imperfection visible. Water damage is the most common ceiling issue in Texas, where summer storms and aging plumbing can send moisture through ceiling panels quickly. A stained, sagging, or cracked ceiling is not just unsightly — it can signal a larger moisture problem that needs to be addressed before the ceiling is repaired.

Wall Doctor TX coordinates experienced ceiling repair crews who handle everything from small water stain repairs to full section replacements. We assess whether the ceiling damage is purely cosmetic or an indicator of active moisture, advise you on next steps, and then deliver a repair that is indistinguishable from the original surface. Texture matching on ceilings — especially popcorn, orange peel, or knockdown — requires a skilled hand to get right.

We serve homeowners, property managers, and investors throughout the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex and Greater Houston area. Whether you're in Arlington, Irving, Pearland, or Conroe, our crews can be scheduled quickly and we'll have a written estimate to you within 1 business day.

Frequently Asked Questions

A sagging ceiling should be taken seriously. A small section sagging from moisture or a few loose fasteners is generally not an immediate danger, but it can fall if ignored. A large section of sagging ceiling, especially if it's still wet and heavy from water damage, poses a real falling hazard. Contact us promptly if you notice significant sagging — we'll assess the situation quickly and let you know the severity and urgency.

Yes — always fix the water source first. Whether it's a plumbing leak, a roof issue, or HVAC condensation, repairing the drywall before the water source is resolved just means you'll be doing it again. We can assess the extent of the ceiling damage and let you know if there are signs of active moisture or mold before we close things up.

Small ceiling repairs (stains, small holes, cracks) can often be completed in a single visit of 2–3 hours. Larger water-damaged sections that require full panel replacement may take 2–3 days total — one day for hanging and taping, then time for mud coats to dry between applications. We'll give you a realistic timeline with your estimate.

Absolutely. We match common Texas ceiling textures including orange peel, knockdown, and smooth finishes. Popcorn ceilings are a special case — see our Popcorn Ceiling Removal page, as matching old popcorn texture can be difficult and many homeowners use the opportunity to remove it entirely and convert to a smooth or modern texture.

Ceiling repair pricing depends on the size of the damaged area, the type of damage, and whether texture matching is needed. A small water stain repair might run $150–$300. Larger section replacements with texture matching typically range from $300–$800+. We provide free written estimates so you know the full cost before any work starts.
