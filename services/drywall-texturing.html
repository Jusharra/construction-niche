Professional Drywall Texturing in Texas

Wall and ceiling texture is one of the most common and most misunderstood elements of a finished interior. In Texas — particularly in DFW and Houston homes built over the past 30 years — orange peel and knockdown textures are the dominant finishes, and matching them during a repair or applying them consistently across a new room requires real skill. The wrong spray pressure, the wrong compound consistency, or the wrong application timing creates a texture that looks patchy, uneven, and obviously wrong.

Wall Doctor TX coordinates experienced texture applicators who handle both spray-applied textures (orange peel, splatter) and hand-applied textures (knockdown, skip trowel, rosebud). For repairs, our technicians test the match on a sample board before touching your wall to ensure the pattern, depth, and density match what's already there. For new construction or remodels, we help you select the right texture for your space and apply it consistently across every surface.

Whether you're repairing a damaged section in a Plano home, re-texturing a remodeled room in Katy, or finishing out a new construction property in McKinney, we deliver textures that look right and hold up long term.

Frequently Asked Questions

Orange peel texture is spray-applied in small, rounded bumps that resemble the skin of an orange. It's consistent, subtle, and very common in Texas residential construction. Knockdown texture starts with a splatter coat that is then "knocked down" with a trowel to flatten the peaks, creating a more irregular, hand-crafted look with both raised and flat areas. Knockdown is more textured and dramatic than orange peel and hides imperfections more effectively.

Matching texture requires matching three things: the pattern, the depth, and the density. We assess your existing texture, adjust compound consistency and spray settings (for spray textures) or tool technique (for hand-applied), and test on a sample board before applying to the repair area. It's not an exact science — it requires experience with the range of variations in common textures. We won't apply until the sample matches.

Spray textures like orange peel are typically dry to the touch within 30–60 minutes and ready for paint primer in 4–8 hours in normal Texas conditions. Hand-applied textures like knockdown and skip trowel may take 12–24 hours to fully dry depending on thickness and humidity. High humidity — common in Houston — extends drying time. We'll give you a specific timeline based on the texture type and current weather conditions.

No — texture must be fully dry before painting. Painting over wet or partially dry texture causes the texture to lift, bubble, or lose adhesion. In Houston's humidity, this is especially important. After texture dries, we also recommend a coat of primer before the finish paint to seal the porous texture surface and prevent blotchy, uneven paint absorption.

If you want to update from an older texture (like popcorn or heavy splatter) to a more modern finish (like light knockdown or smooth), we can remove or skim over the existing texture and apply the new one. For a single-room re-texture, this is typically a 2–3 day process. We'll assess the existing surface and let you know the best approach to get a clean, consistent result.
